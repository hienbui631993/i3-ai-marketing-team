## What is retail shrink — and why is it so hard to stop?
Retail shrink is the gap between the inventory you should have and what you actually
have — lost to theft, fraud, process errors, and administrative mistakes. It's hard to
stop because it's **invisible until you measure it**, and by then the cause is buried in
weeks of footage and POS logs. Shrink remains one of retail's largest and most persistent
margin drains. `[confirm with the latest retail shrink survey before citing a figure]`

## Why is reactive loss prevention failing retailers?
Because it starts *after* the loss. Traditional LP means a small team reviewing recorded
video and POS data after shrink shows up in the numbers — often days or weeks later.
That's slow, manual, and doesn't scale across dozens of sites. The result: most loss is
explained in hindsight, not prevented.

The fix is to flip the model from **reactive** ("what happened?") to **proactive**
("something's happening right now").

## How does AI reduce retail shrink?
AI reduces shrink by watching continuously and flagging the moments that matter — so
your team acts on exceptions instead of scrubbing hours of normal activity. In practice,
that means three things working together:

- **Real-time anomaly alerts** — AI surfaces unusual events at the register, door, or
  stockroom the moment they occur, instead of after the fact.
- **Exception-based reporting** — transactions that look like fraud (refunds, voids,
  no-sales, discounts) are paired with video automatically.
- **Behavioral and traffic analytics** — patterns across a store and across sites reveal
  where and why loss concentrates.

## What is exception-based reporting in loss prevention?
Exception-based reporting (EBR) automatically links suspicious POS events — excessive
refunds, voids, no-sales, manual discounts — to the video of that transaction, so an
investigator reviews **seconds of relevant footage instead of hours**. It's the single
biggest time-saver in modern LP, because it turns "go find the problem" into "here's the
problem, verify it." i3Ai **Smart-ER** is built for exactly this.

## How do you tell internal shrink from external theft?
You correlate **transaction data, video, and store traffic.** External theft tends to
show up as door/floor events without a matching transaction; internal loss shows up in
the POS exceptions tied to specific operators or patterns. Layering **i3Ai Heat Mapping**
on top reveals *where* loss concentrates in the store — so you fix process and placement,
not just chase individuals. Separating the two is what lets you act on the right problem.
