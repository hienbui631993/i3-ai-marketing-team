## Brand tone — "At i3, we speak with purpose."
Four pillars define how every piece of content should feel:

| Pillar | What it means for our writing |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| **Inventive** | Always push the boundaries of what AI and video intelligence can do for real-world businesses. Lead with possibility. |
| **Trustworthy** | Built on **decades of results, not promises.** Back claims with proof; commit to what matters. |
| **Fearless** | Unafraid to challenge outdated systems, rethink the norm, and take on what others avoid. Have a point of view. |
| **Driven** | Motivated by outcomes, by customers' success, and by the belief that good tech should never sit still. |

## Audience (who we write for)
- **Primary:** security directors, loss-prevention managers, IT/operations leaders,
  and business owners — practical, ROI-driven, time-poor, skeptical of hype.
- **Key verticals** (per brand kit secondary-font guidance): **Retail, QSR
  (quick-service restaurants), and Schools**, plus other multi-site businesses.
- **Secondary:** integrators, resellers, and channel partners who deploy i3 solutions.

## Voice — what we sound like
- **Affordable & accessible.** "Affordable" is core positioning — emphasize value/ROI.
- **Trust-first.** We earn belief with decades of results and real outcomes, not buzzwords.
- **Family-minded service.** "Treating every customer like family" — warm, supportive, human.
- **Clear and practical.** Plain English over jargon; always answer the "so what."

## Tone by channel
| Channel | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Blog / articles | Helpful, educational, authoritative-but-warm |
| LinkedIn posts | Professional, insight-led, a little fearless/punchy |
| Email / newsletter | Direct, useful, family-warm, respectful of time |
| Sales decks | Confident, benefit-first, proof-backed |
| Landing pages | Persuasive, scannable, action-oriented |

## Do
- Lead with the customer's problem and the outcome (loss reduced, efficiency up, engagement up).
- Emphasize **affordable, integrated, cloud-based AI** — our differentiators.
- Use concrete numbers/scenarios (shrink %, drive-thru times, # of sites).
- Name the i3 solution, then the benefit (e.g. "i3Ai Sentry → proactive alerts, fewer incidents").
- Write for SEO **and** AI search: question-style headers, structured answers, one CTA.

## Don't
- Don't over-promise or fear-monger — i3 builds **trust**.
- Don't bury benefits under spec-speak.
- Don't use generic AI filler ("In today's fast-paced world…").
- Don't fabricate proof points, customer results, or compliance claims — flag `[confirm]`.

## Vocabulary
- **Prefer:** AI-powered surveillance, business insights, integrated AI solutions,
  affordable, cloud-based, operational efficiency, customer engagement, safety & security.
- **Avoid / use carefully:** "spy", militaristic or dystopian framing (clashes with the
  warm, trust-first, family tone).
