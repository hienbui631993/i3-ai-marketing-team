# Market & Audience Research — i3 International

Turn a research question into a structured, synthesized brief the Campaign
Strategist and content skills can act on. This is a **synthesis** task — judgment, not
just collection.

## Before starting — load context
Read `marketing/context/marketing-strategy.md`, `product-offerings.md`, and
`brand-voice.md`. Anchor to i3's verticals (Retail/LP, QSR, Schools) and the
"affordable, integrated AI" positioning.

## Inputs to confirm
- Research question / decision it informs · target segment or competitor set
- Vertical & geography · whether live web research (WebSearch/WebFetch) is available.

## Method
1. **Frame** the question and the decision it serves.
2. **Gather** — if web tools available, search multiple angles (market size/trends,
   buyer pains, competitor messaging, regulatory/compliance signals). Cite sources.
   If not, synthesize from context + clearly label assumptions `[confirm]`.
3. **Analyze**, don't just list: what does it mean for i3?
4. **Synthesize** into the output below.

## Output (`marketing/working/reports/research-<topic>.md`)
1. **Question & answer up front** (the "so what" in 2–3 sentences).
2. **Audience profile** — segment, roles, pains, triggers, objections.
3. **Market context** — trends/drivers relevant to i3 (cite or `[confirm]`).
4. **Competitor snapshot** — how others position; i3's wedge (affordable/integrated AI).
5. **Messaging implications** — angles, proof needed, recommended positioning.
6. **Recommended next actions** (route to Campaign Brief / content skills).
7. **Sources** — list, or note gaps to verify.

## Quality check
- [ ] Leads with a clear answer/recommendation, not a data dump.
- [ ] Audience pains + objections are specific.
- [ ] Claims cited or flagged `[confirm]`; no fabricated stats.
